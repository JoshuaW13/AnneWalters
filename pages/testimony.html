TESTIMONY OF ANNE WALTERS

ANNE WALTERS was born one stormy wintery day on January 18, 1935 on a farm near Truax, Saskatchewan. Her mother and father, John and Maria Epp, because of the bad weather had to deliver her alone, even without a doctor or midwife.

Anne grew up there and attended the school in Truax until she was age 13, in Grade VII. One day, at the age of 9 her sister, Kathy, led her to the Lord. When she was 13 her family moved to Hawarden, Saskatchewan. She then attended a small country school, Cosmopolitan School, which was situated across the road from our farm. She took Grade VI and VIII there. Then she took Grades IX and X by correspondence with the help of the teacher from the school.

In 1953 when she was 18 years old, she went to Saskatoon to take a Secretarial Course at a vocational Training School. Then she worked for a Law firm for a year.

While attending the Alliance church in Saskatoon at a Missionary conference, she went forward to dedicate her life to the Lord for full-time service, even the mission field. Anne had a strong desire to learn more about the Bible and how to effectively share her faith so in September of 1954 she went to Briercrest Bible Institute.

She attended this school for 3 years, taking any job she could get during the summer to make adequate funds for school and expenses. One summer she worked as a telephone operator in Moose Jaw which was a delightful experience. During two Christmas 2-week breaks, she went with a group of students to work at the Sanatorium at Fort Qu’Appelle.

After graduation in the spring of 1957, she went to work for the Canadian Sunday School Mission as Secretary and also did camp work.

After inquiring Missions Boards, she discovered she needed her High School diploma so in the fall of 1958 she went to Regina College to take a condensed High School course and got her diploma after much hard work.

It was while she was there, that she met Alvin Walters, who was there for the same reason. They became interested in each other and sensed God’s leading in their relationship and in June of 1959 they were engaged. In June 1960 they were married in Saskatoon, Saskatchewan.

Al had already started ministry in a small work at Horseshoe Bay, B.C. and so at this time, Anne joined him and they served the Lord together there.

They were blessed with four children during their life together: namely, Debra (1963), Denise (1965), Deanne (1967), and Dale (1971). What a joy and blessing these children were to them. The were also blessed with many grandchildren: Mitchell (1989), Matthew (1991), Jonathon (1995), Jeffrey (1995), Rachel (1998), Jayson (1999), Simon (1999), Joshua (2002), Kaylin (2004) and Rebekkah (2006).

Every time they applied or inquired about Missions, the doors closed so they continued ministry in many churches: such as Revelstoke, B.C., Trail, B.C., Penticton, B.C. Drumheller, AB, Kindersley, Sask., North Battleford, Sask.. In 1984 they moved to Fort Macleod to church plant, something they both had a desire to do. It was a very slow, hard work for about 10 years and then growth came, they saw people come, accept the Lord and become a part of a great group of people. In 1999 they helped build a church building which was a great accomplishment. The church dedication occurred in May, 2000 and they then retired and moved to High River, AB in June 2000. They continued in part-time ministry through their retirement years as they were able. The Lord was so good to Anne and Alvin through their years of service in both leading and supplying their every need abundantly.
